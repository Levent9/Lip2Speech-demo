Demos from "ZERO-SHOT PERSONALIZED LIP-TO-SPEECH SYNTHESIS WITH FACE IMAGE BASED VOICE CONTROL"

Paper: TO DO

Authors: Zheng-Yan Sheng, Zhen-Hua-Ling

Abstract: Lip-to-Speech (Lip2Speech) synthesis, which predicts the corresponding audio given talking face images, has witnessed significant progress with various training strategies and models in a series of independent works. However, these works can not achieve voice control under zero-shot condition, that extra speaker embedding needs to be extracted from the actual reference speech, which is unavailable from silent video input. In addition, most of these works ignore the match between voice characteristics and face for unseen speaker’s speech synthesis. In this paper, we propose zero-shot personalized Lip2Speech synthesis, where the face image controls speaker identity. Variational autoencoder is introduced to our framework for disentangling the speaker identity and linguistic content information, that enables voice characteristics controlled by the speaker embedding. Furthermore, we propose associated cross-modal representation learning to promote the control ability of face-based speaker embedding (FSE) to voice characteristics, supporting the above Lip2Speech process. Extensive experiments verify the effectiveness of the proposed method that synthesized unseen speaker’s utterances are more natural and matched with face compared with the other methods. To our best knowledge we are the first to perform zero-shot personalized Lip2Speech synthesis with a face image rather than actual reference audio to control voice characteristics.
